For most people, you will vote in the riding where you live. This riding, the Vancouver-Quadra riding, encompasses UBC, Point Grey, Musqueam Nation, Kerrisdale, Marpole, and Kitsilano to Arbutus Street. If you are a student, you may be unsure of which riding you "live" in. Maybe you live on campus, or you live with roommates but you grew up elsewhere in the city or Canada. The answer is: whichever place you want to call home. If you live on residence, you can get your residence administrator to give you a Letter of Confirmation of Residence. If you live off campus, you just need a valid bill (cell phone, internet, Hydro) with your address on it. For more information: Elections Canada Student Information

Elections Canada keeps and updates a database of eligible voters, using information from the CRA, ICBC, etc., so it is likely you are registered. You need to be registered in order to vote, and you can check if you are registered here: Elections Canada Registration

A more detailed information page can be found here: Elections Canada Ways to Vote

Polling Station
If you have received your voter information card, your voting station will be listed there. Otherwise, you can check the Voter Information Service after October 2.
You can also vote from October 11 - October 14 in Advanced Voting at your voting station.

Elections Canada Office
You may also vote anytime before October 15 at an Elections Canada Office. The Elections Canada Office in the Vancouver-Quadra Riding is:

Broadway Pacific
2106 Broadway West
Vancouver BC V6K2C8

Mail-In Ballot
To vote by mail, you need to register with Elections Canada by October 15: Elections Canada Vote by Mail

You will need either: 1 piece of government-issued ID with current address (e.g. Driver's License)
OR
1 piece of ID with proof of address (such as a Hydro bill) AND
1 piece of ID with your name (e.g. Student ID)

The Elections Canada website has more details on what ID you can use, and what to do if you don't have ID: Elections Canada ID Information
